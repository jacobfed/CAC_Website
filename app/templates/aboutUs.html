We envision a community where children are safe, families are resilient, and those affected by child abuse are empowered to heal and thrive.

What is a Child Advocacy Center? It is a national best practice model utilized to reduce potential trauma to children and families impacted by child abuse and improve services through interagency collaboration. The overall goal, is to send the child and family to one child-friendly, trauma-informed, location where they will receive a wide array of services to help in both the short and long-term healing in a prompt and thorough manner. Additionally, there is a goal to aid in improving prosecutions.

Components of a CAC include medical/SANE exams, child protection, law enforcement, victim advocacy, mental health/trauma-informed response, prosecution, and allied community and social services. The partners work in close collaboration to ensure that there is timely and appropriate responses to the child and their families.

For the last few years in Cuyahoga County, a multi-disciplinary advisory committee representing the key responders in a CAC has been meeting for a few years to be bring this model to this area.

We are members of the Ohio Network of Child Advocacy Centers Network.
